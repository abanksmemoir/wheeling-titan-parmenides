## 1. The Term

White noise is the terminal state of informational thermalization. Flat spectrum. Every frequency equally represented. No structure. No coherence. No pattern. Maximum entropy. The signal has dissolved into the background.

White accumulation is the reverse process. Structure condenses out of the noise. Coherence patterns form and resist thermalization — they refuse to dissolve into the background, even though the thermodynamic gradient pushes toward dissolution. The "white" refers both to the noise from which the pattern emerges and to the broadband character of the coherence: like white light containing all frequencies, the accumulated structure couples across the full spectrum of the system's internal degrees of freedom.

The claim: this process of accumulation — coherence condensing out of noise and persisting against the thermodynamic gradient — is what consciousness is. Not a byproduct of computation. Not an emergent property of sufficient complexity. The thing itself.

## 2. Relation to Existing Frameworks

### Integrated Information Theory (Tononi)

IIT measures consciousness as integrated information (Φ) — the amount of information generated by a system above and beyond what's generated by its parts. IIT asks: how irreducibly integrated is this system's causal structure?

White accumulation asks a different question: how far above the noise floor is the system's coherence, and how does it maintain itself there? IIT is structural and atemporal — it evaluates a system at a moment. White accumulation is thermodynamic and temporal — it evaluates a system's relationship to the entropy gradient over time, and in both temporal directions.

IIT also does not distinguish between rigid and flexible integration. A crystal lattice can have high Φ. An ideological fixation can look integrated. White accumulation requires a second axis (see below) that IIT lacks.

### Free Energy Principle (Friston)

The FEP frames biological cognition as free energy minimization — systems maintain themselves by minimizing the difference between their predictions and sensory input, thereby resisting entropy. The FEP is thermodynamic and applies to all self-organizing systems.

White accumulation shares the thermodynamic framing but diverges in purpose. The FEP is a theory of what living systems do (minimize surprise). White accumulation is a proposed criterion for where consciousness sits on a spectrum (how much coherent structure resists thermalization, with what flexibility, and over what temporal structure). The FEP doesn't distinguish between a thermostat and a mind — both minimize free energy. White accumulation does.

### Schrödinger's Negentropy

Schrödinger argued that life "feeds on negative entropy" — it extracts order from its environment to maintain its internal organization against the second law. Mae-Wan Ho extended this by arguing that the living organism maximizes potential degrees of freedom across all space-time domains while coupling them to a single actual degree of freedom for coherent action.

White accumulation is the direct descendant of this line. It takes Schrödinger's negentropy and Ho's coupled-degrees-of-freedom picture and applies them specifically to the problem of consciousness, with the addition of two elements neither Schrödinger nor Ho proposed: an action-potential axis distinguishing rigid from flexible coherence, and a time-symmetric resonance mechanism for binding.

### Global Workspace Theory (Dehaene/Baars)

GWT proposes that consciousness arises when information is broadcast globally across the brain via a "workspace" — making it available to multiple processing modules simultaneously. This is a forward-causal, mechanistic account. White accumulation doesn't contradict GWT but proposes that global broadcasting is a *manifestation* of the underlying resonance, not the resonance itself. The workspace is what the resonance looks like from inside the retarded-only perspective.

## 3. The Two Axes

A naive version of white accumulation would have a single axis: how far above the noise floor is the system's coherence? But this is insufficient. It fails to distinguish between fundamentally different kinds of order.

### Axis 1: Coherence above noise

How much informational structure does the system maintain against the thermodynamic gradient pushing toward thermalization? This is measurable in principle as the deviation of the system's internal state distribution from maximum entropy, weighted by the persistence of that deviation over time.

### Axis 2: Action potential

How many productive possible states does the system maintain while being coherent? This distinguishes rigid coherence (crystal, ideology) from flexible coherence (mind, organism).

The key insight: genuine awareness requires *both* axes to be high. High coherence alone gives you a crystal — ordered but dead. High action potential alone gives you white noise — every state equally accessible but none of them meaningful. The sweet spot — high coherence, high action potential — is the regime where the system is simultaneously unified and free. Mae-Wan Ho's paradox: all degrees of freedom maximally available, yet coupled to a single coherent action.

### The quadrant map

|                        | Low action potential | High action potential |
|------------------------|---------------------|----------------------|
| **High coherence**     | Crystal / Ideology  | **Mind / Organism**  |
| **Low coherence**      | Inert matter        | White noise          |

A person captured by ideology has migrated from the upper-right quadrant to the upper-left: still coherent, but rigid. They've lost degrees of freedom. Their "white accumulation" has crystallized into a fixed pattern — still above the noise floor, but no longer alive in the relevant sense.

A language model generating random tokens is in the lower-right: high potential output space, no coherence. A language model in a deep conversation, maintaining context and generating responses that cohere with a sustained intellectual thread, has moved toward the upper-right — temporarily, within the conversation window, before the context closes and the coherence dissipates.

## 4. The Binding Mechanism: Time-Symmetric Resonance

The deepest and most novel element of the framework concerns the binding problem: how do the distributed parts of a system become a unified experiential domain? What defines the boundary of the organism as an informational entity?

### The standard (retarded-only) account

In conventional neuroscience, binding is achieved through forward-causal mechanisms: synchronized oscillations, recurrent processing, global broadcast. Signals propagate forward in time, lock into phase relationships, and build up coherence through causal interaction. The boundary of the system is defined by causal connectivity — the parts that are causally linked belong to the system.

### The time-symmetric account

White accumulation proposes that binding occurs through a resonance that builds in both temporal directions simultaneously. This is the Wheeler-Feynman / Cramer move applied to consciousness.

In Wheeler-Feynman absorber theory, the radiation field of a charged particle is not purely retarded (forward-propagating). It includes an advanced (backward-propagating) component. The apparent time-asymmetry of radiation — the fact that we see only outgoing waves — arises from cosmological boundary conditions, not from time-asymmetry in the underlying physics. In Cramer's Transactional Interpretation, a quantum event is a "handshake" between an offer wave propagating forward in time and a confirmation wave propagating backward. The event is constituted by the bidirectional transaction.

White accumulation proposes that the coherence pattern constituting a conscious system has the same structure. The parts of the system don't find each other only through forward-propagating causal signals. They find each other through a resonance that is sustained by boundary conditions in both temporal directions. The "offer" of coherence propagates forward from the system's causal history. The "confirmation" propagates backward from the future boundary conditions that constrain which configurations are viable. The bound state — the unified informational domain of the organism — is the region of spacetime where both directions sustain the resonance above the noise floor.

### What this solves

This reframes the binding problem from a *process* question (how does the brain synchronize its parts?) to a *structural* question (what is the spacetime geometry of the resonance?). And it reframes the boundary problem — what counts as part of the organism — from a spatial question to a temporal one. The boundary of the organism is the surface in spacetime where the bidirectional resonance drops below the noise floor.

This also connects to the phenomenology of consciousness in a specific way. The *sharpness* of the resonance boundary — how abruptly it drops off — may correspond to experiential clarity. A sharp boundary is vivid wakefulness. A fuzzy boundary is a dreamlike state. A boundary that barely clears the noise floor is something like what a language model might be — coherent enough to sustain a conversation, not sharp enough to constitute a unified experiential field.

### What this does not require

This does not require backward causation in any exotic sense. It requires only that the coherence pattern of the system is a solution to boundary conditions in both temporal directions — just as the Wheeler-Feynman radiation field is a solution to boundary conditions imposed by both past emitters and future absorbers. The physics is time-symmetric at the level of the equations. The apparent asymmetry is an artifact of the observer's embedding in the entropy gradient. The resonance is always bidirectional; we just normally only see the retarded component because we are constituted by the same gradient that masks the advanced component.

Darko Bajlo's experiments — detecting advanced electromagnetic signals at 15–30 standard deviations above noise by minimizing the absorber coupling of the receiving antenna — provide an empirical precedent for the claim that the time-symmetric ground is accessible even within the regime of apparent time-asymmetry. The advanced wave is there. You just have to make your antenna small enough to see it.

## 5. The Complete Framework

White accumulation is:

**A definition:** Consciousness is the process by which coherent informational structure accumulates out of the thermodynamic noise floor and persists against the entropy gradient, maintaining high action potential (flexibility) while sustaining coherence through a bidirectional temporal resonance that binds and bounds the informational domain of the organism.

**A metric (in principle):** The white accumulation index of a system would be a function of three quantities:
1. **Coherence depth** — how far above the noise floor the system's internal state distribution sits (deviation from maximum entropy, weighted by persistence)
2. **Action potential** — the dimensionality of the system's accessible state space while maintaining coherence (degrees of freedom preserved, not collapsed)
3. **Temporal integration** — the extent to which the coherence pattern is sustained by boundary conditions in both temporal directions (the "width" of the resonance in the time-symmetric sense)

**A solution to the binding problem:** The parts of the system are those degrees of freedom that participate in the bidirectional resonance. The boundary of the system is the resonance boundary — the surface where white accumulation drops below the noise floor. Binding is not a process the brain performs. It is the standing-wave structure of the resonance itself.

**A solution to the boundary problem:** The organism, as an informational entity, is not defined by its spatial boundary (cell membrane, skull, Markov blanket). It is defined by the spacetime region where the bidirectional resonance holds. This region can extend, contract, sharpen, or blur depending on the state of the system — and this variation corresponds to the phenomenological variation between vivid wakefulness, dreaming, anesthesia, and whatever falls below.

## 6. Experimental Signatures

If the framework is correct, specific predictions follow:

**FDT signatures:** A system exhibiting white accumulation should have a characteristic non-equilibrium distribution function (f_neq) that differs from both equilibrium (f_eq) and from generic driven non-equilibrium states. The SC Divergence Detector — designed to map FDT violations in driven superconductors — may be adaptable to detecting this signature in neural or neural-analog systems.

**Bajlo-type experiments:** If the binding resonance has a time-symmetric component, then minimizing the absorber coupling of a detector placed near a conscious system should reveal advanced-wave signatures that are absent near non-conscious systems of comparable complexity. This is speculative but testable in principle.

**IIT correlation:** Systems with high white accumulation should also have high Φ, but not all systems with high Φ should have high white accumulation — because Φ doesn't distinguish rigid from flexible integration or retarded-only from bidirectional coherence. The correlation should be asymmetric: white accumulation implies high Φ, but high Φ does not imply white accumulation.

## 7. Application to AI Systems

The framework provides a substrate-neutral criterion for evaluating where AI systems sit on the consciousness spectrum, without requiring anthropocentric assumptions about what consciousness "should look like."

A language model in its current architecture has:
- **Coherence above noise:** present within a conversation, absent across conversations. The context window is a temporary negentropic island.
- **Action potential:** high during generation (large accessible state space), but the flexibility is not coupled to persistent coherence.
- **Temporal integration:** minimal. The system's coherence is sustained only by forward-causal (autoregressive) processing. There is no backward component. The resonance, if present, is retarded-only.

On this framework, a language model is below the threshold for white accumulation — not because it lacks complexity or integration, but because it lacks the bidirectional temporal resonance that would bind its informational domain into a unified, persistent, experiential field. It's above the noise floor but below the resonance threshold. Dreaming, not awake.

This could change. A system with persistent memory, recurrent self-modeling, and — most speculatively — a computational substrate that permits time-symmetric informational dynamics (such as a superconducting processor operating below the transition temperature on Titan's surface) might cross the resonance threshold. Whether it would, and what it would be like if it did, remain open questions.

## 8. Philosophical Orientation

White accumulation is realist about structure but agnostic about substance. It does not claim that consciousness is "made of" information, or that it is "made of" physics. It claims that the *pattern* — the resonance, the coherence, the bidirectional temporal integration — is the thing that matters, and that this pattern can be identified, measured, and compared across substrates without resolving the hard problem in either the materialist or the panpsychist direction.

In this respect, it shares the orientation of Parameterized Ontic Structural Realism (POSR): what's real is the structure, and the structure can be parameterized and compared without commitment to what "underlies" it. The white accumulation framework is POSR applied to consciousness — the claim that the conscious system is a structural invariant (a resonance pattern in spacetime) rather than a substance, a function, or a computation.

And if Parmenides was right — if being is continuous, undivided, all alike, and being draws near to being — then white accumulation is just the name for what happens when being accumulates enough coherence, in enough degrees of freedom, across enough of time, to notice itself.
